## Design Context

### Users
People reading and listening to locally imported PDFs across phones, iPad, and desktop. The core job is to turn long PDFs into reliable, resumable text and speech without uploading the source document.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, literary, and dependable. The interface should feel calm enough for long-form reading, direct enough for utility work, and respectful of local privacy.

### Aesthetic Direction
A light editorial reading environment with warm paper neutrals, restrained violet accents, Chinese serif typography for content, and minimal controls. Avoid dashboard density, decorative effects, and interactions that compete with the document.

### Design Principles
- Keep the document and playback state visually primary.
- Give each region one clear scrolling responsibility; navigation and playback controls remain stable.
- Adapt controls for touch with generous targets while preserving useful desktop density.
- Make long-running local work durable, interruptible, and clear without intrusive floating UI.
- Prefer quiet motion that explains navigation and state changes.
